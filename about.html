Our Story

Our History

United Shalom Pentecostal Church Manchester was founded by a group of faithful believers who carried the same vision and passion as the wider USPC family — to see lives transformed by the power of the Holy Spirit in cities across the United Kingdom.

What began as a small gathering has grown into a vibrant, multicultural congregation in the heart of Manchester. God has been faithful in every season, and we continue to stand on His promises as we grow and reach out to our community.

As part of the United Shalom Pentecostal Church network, we share the same values, doctrine, and mission that have shaped USPC churches across the country — rooted in Scripture, powered by the Spirit, and moved by love.

Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To see Manchester transformed by the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

We envision a city where the love of God is felt in every neighbourhood, every family, and every heart. A Manchester where people know they are known, loved, and valued by God.

Our Mission

To share God's love, strengthen faith, and build a united community.

Through worship, discipleship, fellowship, and outreach, we are committed to making disciples of all nations — starting right here in Manchester — baptising them in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Our Doctrine

What We Believe

We are a Pentecostal church rooted in the authority of Scripture and the full gospel of Jesus Christ.

The Holy Scriptures

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible Word of God, our supreme guide in all matters of faith and conduct.

The Trinity

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Salvation

We believe in salvation through repentance and faith in Jesus Christ, who died for our sins and rose again.

Water Baptism

We believe in water baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith in Jesus Christ.

Baptism of the Holy Spirit

We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit with the evidence of speaking in other tongues, as on the Day of Pentecost.

Divine Healing

We believe in healing for spirit, soul, and body through faith in Jesus Christ, who bore our sicknesses.

The Second Coming

We believe in the literal, physical return of Jesus Christ to establish His kingdom on earth.

The Church

We believe the church is the body of Christ, called to worship God and make disciples of all nations.
